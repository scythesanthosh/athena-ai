How to analyse your Cognitive Report?

A scholarly analysis of your mental landscape, designed for the refined mind seeking clarity and cognitive mastery.

01

Overall Trend

This section describes the general pattern across all entries that were analyzed. It tells you whether your thoughts: Are becoming more positive or negative, show emotional stability or volatility, or contain repeated thinking styles.

02

Dominant Cognitive Patterns

This section lists the thinking styles that appear most frequently in your entries. Common examples include:

Catastrophizing

assuming the worst possible outcome.

Overgeneralization

drawing broad conclusions from a single event.

Emotional reasoning

believing something is true because it feels true.

Grandiose self-concept

viewing oneself in exaggerated or extreme roles.

Identity fusion

strongly identifying with a particular idea, emotion, or role.

These patterns are not judgments, but signals about how thoughts may be structured.

03

Distortion Analysis

This section measures how often cognitive distortions appear. Cognitive distortions are biased thinking patterns that can make situations feel worse or more extreme than they are. Higher frequency suggests the mind may be interpreting events through strong emotional filters.

04

Emotional Trend

This part examines the emotional tone of your entries. It tracks: Intensity of emotions, stability vs volatility, and shifts between positive and negative states.

05

Key Observations

This section lists specific examples from your entries that influenced the analysis to ensure transparency.

  • Emotional expressions
  • Descriptions of experiences
  • Strong statements or imagery
06

Positive Improvements

This section highlights constructive or positive signals in your entries. Even if negative patterns appear, this section shows areas of resilience and progress.

07

Strategies

Some Recommended Strategies: Thought-record worksheets, grounding techniques, and gratitude journaling. These are general well-being practices, not medical prescriptions.

08

Attention Required

Critical Observation: High-risk indicators detected.

Expressions of self-harm, extreme hopelessness, or persistent negative thinking. This section encourages extra care and possible professional support if necessary.

09

Cognitive Archetype

Types

The Strategist

A structured, forward-thinking mindset focused on analyzing situations, anticipating outcomes, and planning solutions.

The Self-Critic

A thinking style marked by strong self-evaluation and internal judgment.

The Catastrophizer

Patterns where situations are interpreted through worst-case outcomes or extreme consequences.

The Stoic Processor

A calm and controlled cognitive style that processes experiences rationally with limited emotional expression.

The Idealist

A value-driven mindset focused on meaning, principles, and aspirational thinking.

The Reactor

A style characterized by strong immediate emotional responses that shape interpretation of experiences.

The Comparer

A tendency to evaluate oneself relative to others or external standards.

The Optimizer

A mindset oriented toward continuous improvement and refining systems or behaviors.
